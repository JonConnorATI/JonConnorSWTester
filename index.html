About me

Most of my working life has been involved in testing. I started off in Quality Control and worked for manufacturers of sandpaper, adhesive labels, aluminium fabrications, and turbine generators, from my hometown of Manchester to the sleepy village of Arcen in The Netherlands. Then I spent over 10 years working in Environmental Emissions testing, whilst I was still working in the field, I got more involved in project management, resourcing, scheduling, sales, and invoicing.
However, after a second redundancy I decided to get involved in software testing and I wish I had done it a lot sooner. They say, “if you work at something you enjoy then you will never work a day in your life.” I agree with that about 80% of the time but it doesn’t always hold true at 6pm on a Friday.
In the 5 years that I've been working as a software tester I really enjoy being exposed to new technology (new to me, anyway) and learning new skills.
